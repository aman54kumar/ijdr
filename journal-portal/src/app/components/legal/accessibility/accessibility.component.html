Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to making IJDR accessible to all users

Last updated: January 1, 2025

1. Our Commitment

The Indian Journal of Development Research (IJDR) is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

2. Accessibility Standards

We strive to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities.

Standards We Follow

  • WCAG 2.1 Level AA: International accessibility guidelines
  • Section 508: U.S. federal accessibility standards
  • EN 301 549: European accessibility standard
  • AODA: Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act

3. Accessibility Features

Navigation and Structure

  • Logical heading structure for screen readers
  • Keyboard navigation support
  • Skip links to main content
  • Consistent navigation across all pages
  • Breadcrumb navigation for orientation

Visual Design

  • High contrast color schemes
  • Scalable fonts and text
  • Clear visual focus indicators
  • Consistent layout and design patterns
  • Alternative text for images

Content

  • Clear and simple language
  • Descriptive link text
  • Structured data markup
  • Accessible PDF documents
  • Captions for video content

4. Assistive Technology Support

Our website is designed to work with assistive technologies including:

  • Screen Readers: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Voice Recognition: Dragon NaturallySpeaking
  • Keyboard Navigation: Full keyboard accessibility
  • Magnification: ZoomText, built-in browser zoom
  • Switch Navigation: Switch control devices

5. Testing and Evaluation

Regular Testing

We conduct regular accessibility testing using:

  • Automated accessibility testing tools
  • Manual testing with screen readers
  • Keyboard-only navigation testing
  • Color contrast analysis
  • User testing with people with disabilities

Third-Party Audits

We engage accessibility consultants to perform comprehensive audits and provide recommendations for improvement.

6. Known Issues and Limitations

We are continuously working to improve accessibility. Current known issues include:

  • Some older PDF documents may not be fully accessible
  • Third-party embedded content may not meet accessibility standards
  • Complex data tables may need additional navigation aids

Ongoing Improvements

We are actively working to address these issues and will provide updates as improvements are implemented.

7. Alternative Formats

If you need content in an alternative format, we can provide:

  • Large print versions
  • Plain text formats
  • Audio recordings
  • Braille documents (upon request)
  • Simplified language versions

8. Feedback and Support

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of IJDR. Please let us know if you:

  • Encounter accessibility barriers
  • Have suggestions for improvement
  • Need content in an alternative format
  • Require assistance accessing information

Response Time

We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 2 business days and resolve issues within 10 business days when possible.
